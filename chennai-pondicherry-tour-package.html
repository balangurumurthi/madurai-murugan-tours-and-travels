The route

The East Coast Road — India's most scenic coastal highway

The ECR (East Coast Road) stretches 160 km from Chennai to Pondicherry, hugging the Bay of Bengal coastline. Crystal-blue waters on one side, casuarina groves on the other — with temple towns, surf beaches and seafood shacks dotting the way. Our drivers know every photo stop, every shortcut, and the best places to pull over for filter coffee and fresh-caught fish.

Day-by-day itinerary

Chennai to Pondicherry — your 2–3 day coastal adventure

A carefully planned itinerary covering heritage, beaches, spirituality and French-colonial charm. Flexible — customise any stop to match your pace.

Day 1 — Chennai to Pondicherry via ECR

Morning · 7:00 AM

Pickup from Chennai

Our driver picks you up from your hotel, airport or railway station in Chennai. Start the drive south on the East Coast Road (ECR). The coastal views begin within 30 minutes of leaving the city.

Mid-morning · 9:00 AM

Mahabalipuram — UNESCO World Heritage stop

Stop at the iconic Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance and Pancha Rathas — Pallava-era rock-cut monuments right by the sea. Spend 1.5–2 hours exploring. Great photo opportunities at sunrise lighting.

Late morning · 11:00 AM

Crocodile Bank

Quick stop at the Madras Crocodile Bank Trust — home to over 2,000 crocodiles, alligators and gharials. A fun 30–40 minute visit, especially for families with kids.

Afternoon · 1:30 PM

Arrive Pondicherry — lunch & hotel check-in

Arrive in Pondicherry after the scenic ECR drive. Check into your hotel and enjoy a relaxed lunch at one of the French-Tamil fusion restaurants. Try the local seafood or crepes.

Evening · 4:30 PM

French Quarter evening walk

Stroll through the charming French Quarter (White Town) — pastel-coloured colonial buildings, bougainvillea-draped streets, quaint cafes, the Aurobindo Ashram, and the Sacred Heart Basilica.

Sunset · 6:00 PM

Promenade Beach sunset

End Day 1 with a glorious sunset at Promenade Beach (Rock Beach). Walk along the 1.5 km seaside promenade, visit the Gandhi statue, and enjoy street food from the evening vendors.

Day 2 — Auroville, beaches & Pondicherry exploration

Morning · 8:00 AM

Auroville — Matrimandir viewing

Visit Auroville, the experimental international township. Walk through the lush Banyan tree gardens to the Matrimandir viewing point — the iconic golden sphere. Allow 1.5–2 hours. Carry your passport/ID.

Late morning · 10:30 AM

Paradise Beach

Take a boat ride to the secluded Paradise Beach (Plage Paradiso). Crystal-clear waters, white sand, and almost no crowds on weekdays. Perfect for swimming and relaxing. 1–1.5 hours.

Midday · 12:30 PM

Serenity Beach

Head to Serenity Beach — a favourite among surfers. Watch the fishing boats come in, try your hand at surfing (lessons available), or simply enjoy the rugged coastal beauty.

Afternoon · 2:00 PM

Pondicherry Museum

Explore the Pondicherry Museum housing French-colonial artefacts, Roman antiquities from Arikamedu, Chola bronzes and geological specimens. A compact but fascinating 45-minute visit.

Evening · 4:30 PM

Goubert Market shopping & French cuisine dinner

Browse Goubert Market for local handicrafts, Auroville products, aromatic oils and incense. End the day with an authentic French-Tamil dinner — think bouillabaisse with a South Indian twist.

Day 3 (Optional) — Chidambaram side trip & return

Morning · 7:30 AM

Chidambaram Nataraja Temple

Drive 60 km south from Pondicherry to the ancient Chidambaram Nataraja Temple — one of the Pancha Bootha Sthalams dedicated to Lord Shiva as the cosmic dancer. The temple's architecture and the Chit Sabha (hall of consciousness) are awe-inspiring. Allow 2 hours.

Late morning · 11:00 AM

Pichavaram Mangrove Forest (optional add-on)

If time permits, take a short detour to Pichavaram — the world's second-largest mangrove forest. Boat through the serene backwaters surrounded by mangrove canopy. A 1-hour boat ride.

Afternoon · 1:00 PM

Return drive to Chennai

Begin the return journey to Chennai via ECR or the faster NH32 (via Tindivanam). Arrive Chennai by evening (5–6 PM). Drop-off at hotel, airport or railway station.

Common questions

Chennai to Pondicherry tour — FAQ

How long is the ECR drive from Chennai to Pondicherry?
The East Coast Road (ECR) from Chennai to Pondicherry is approximately 160 km and takes 3.5 to 4 hours with stops at Mahabalipuram, Crocodile Bank and photo points along the Bay of Bengal coast. Without stops, the drive takes about 2.5 to 3 hours. We recommend taking the scenic route with stops — it's one of India's best coastal drives.
What is the best season to visit Pondicherry from Chennai?
The best time to visit Pondicherry is October to March when the weather is pleasant (22–30 °C) and ideal for beach activities and sightseeing. The monsoon months (November–December) can bring heavy rain but also lush green landscapes. Weekdays are less crowded than weekends year-round. Summer (April–June) is hot but you'll find fewer tourists and better hotel rates.
Do you arrange hotel stays in Pondicherry?
Yes. We offer car + hotel combo packages with budget, mid-range and premium options in Pondicherry. We partner with trusted hotels in the French Quarter, White Town and near the beaches. Heritage boutique stays, beachfront resorts and budget guesthouses are all available. WhatsApp us your preference and budget.
Is an inter-state permit required for Pondicherry?
Yes. Pondicherry is a Union Territory and vehicles registered in Tamil Nadu require an inter-state permit. We handle the permit for you — the permit fee (approximately ₹200–300) is charged as an extra along with toll and parking. You don't need to worry about the paperwork.
Can we customise the Pondicherry itinerary?
Absolutely. The itinerary is fully flexible. You can skip Auroville and spend more time at beaches, add a Chidambaram temple visit, include Pichavaram mangrove boating, or extend the trip to 3 days. Tell us your preferences on WhatsApp and we will plan a personalised itinerary at no extra planning cost.
