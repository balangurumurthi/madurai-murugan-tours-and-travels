Day-by-day itinerary

Chennai to Tirupati — your pilgrimage plan

Choose between a focused 1-day darshan trip or a relaxed 2-day temple circuit covering Srikalahasti and Kanipakam.

Day 1 · 3:00 AM

Early morning departure from Chennai

We pick you up from your home, hotel, or Chennai airport at 3:00 AM (recommended). Early departure ensures you beat the Tirumala rush and reach before the morning darshan crowd builds up. The highway is smooth and empty at this hour.

Day 1 · 6:00 AM

Arrive at Tirumala — 135 km via NH48

Reach Tirumala via the ghat road (about 3 hours from Chennai). Our driver navigates the 18 hairpin bends with ease. Quick freshening up at the guesthouse or TTD facilities before heading to the temple.

Day 1 · 7:00 AM – 11:00 AM

Sri Venkateswara (Balaji) Temple darshan

The main event — darshan at the sacred Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanam. With ₹300 Special Entry Darshan (SED), expect 1–2 hours in queue. Free darshan (Sarva Darshan) can take 4–10 hours depending on the day. We recommend SED tickets booked online in advance.

Day 1 · 12:00 PM

Padmavathi Temple, Tiruchanoor

After Balaji darshan, drive down to Tiruchanoor (5 km from Tirupati town) to visit the Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple — dedicated to Lord Balaji's consort. Traditionally, pilgrims visit Padmavathi Temple to complete the Tirupati pilgrimage. Darshan here is quicker, usually 30–45 minutes.

Day 1 · 2:00 PM (1-day package)

Return drive to Chennai

If you chose the 1-day package: After lunch at a local restaurant, begin the return drive to Chennai. Reach Chennai by 5:00–6:00 PM, completing the entire pilgrimage in a single day. Perfect for devotees with limited time.

Day 1 · Evening (2-day package)

Overnight stay near Tirupati

If you chose the 2-day package: Check in to a hotel near Tirupati town. Rest after the long day of darshan. We can arrange budget, mid-range or premium hotel stays. Explore the local market for Tirupati laddus and souvenirs.

Day 2 · 7:00 AM

Srikalahasti Temple

Drive to Srikalahasteeswara Temple (40 km from Tirupati, ~45 min). One of the Pancha Bhuta Sthalams (Wind element), this ancient Shiva temple is famous for Rahu-Ketu pooja and Kala Sarpa Dosha Nivarana. Darshan takes about 1–2 hours.

Day 2 · 10:30 AM

Kanipakam Vinayagar Temple

Head to Kanipakam (about 70 km from Srikalahasti) to visit the famous Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple. The self-manifested Ganesha idol here is believed to be growing in size. A serene temple with a beautiful tank. Darshan takes about 45 minutes.

Day 2 · 1:00 PM

ISKCON Temple, Tirupati

Optional stop at the ISKCON Sri Sri Radha Govinda Mandir in Tirupati. A beautifully maintained modern temple with peaceful surroundings. Great for a quiet visit before the return journey. Prasadam available.

Day 2 · 2:30 PM

Return drive to Chennai

After lunch, begin the return drive to Chennai via NH48. Arrive in Chennai by 5:30–6:30 PM. Drop-off at your home, hotel, airport or railway station.

Essential information

Important tips for Tirupati darshan

Know before you go — darshan types, dress code, what to carry, and restrictions at Tirumala Temple.

Darshan booking types

₹300 Special Entry Darshan (SED) — shorter queue, 1–2 hrs. Book online on the TTD website 2–3 days in advance. Free darshan (Sarva Darshan) — walk-in, but expect 4–10 hour queues. VIP darshan available for senior citizens and differently abled persons.

Dress code

Traditional attire is mandatory. Men: dhoti/veshti or formal trousers with shirt. Women: saree, churidar, or salwar kameez. Not allowed: jeans, shorts, sleeveless tops, T-shirts with prints, and leather belts. Dress conservatively to avoid being turned away at the gate.

Mobile phone restrictions

Mobile phones, cameras, and all electronic gadgets are strictly prohibited inside the temple. Leave them in the car with the driver or use TTD locker facilities (₹50–100). Our driver will safeguard your belongings while you are inside.

What to carry

Must have: ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN), darshan ticket printout, minimal cash. Optional: small towel (if tonsuring), change of clothes, prasadam box. Do not carry: large bags, food items, footwear inside the queue complex (use designated stands).

Route details

Chennai to Tirupati — route & distance

135 km via NH48

The fastest route from Chennai to Tirupati is via NH48 (Chennai–Tiruttani–Tirupati highway). Well-maintained 4-lane road for most of the journey. The Tirumala ghat road has 18 hairpin bends — our drivers know every curve.

Approx 3 hours drive

Chennai to Tirupati town takes about 2.5–3 hours. Add another 30–40 minutes for the ghat road climb to Tirumala. Total driving time one-way: approximately 3–3.5 hours depending on traffic and ghat road conditions.

Inter-state crossing

You cross from Tamil Nadu into Andhra Pradesh near Tada/Sullurpeta. Our vehicles carry the required inter-state permit arranged in advance. The border crossing is seamless — no stops or checkpoints for tourist vehicles.

Frequently asked questions

Chennai to Tirupati tour — FAQ

What are the darshan types at Tirumala Balaji Temple?
TTD offers multiple darshan types. ₹300 Special Entry Darshan (SED) gives you a shorter queue of 1–2 hours — book online on the TTD website at least 2–3 days in advance. Free darshan (Sarva Darshan) is walk-in but can take 4–10 hours depending on the day and season. VIP Break Darshan is available for senior citizens (65+) and differently abled persons with valid proof.
Why do you recommend a 3 AM start from Chennai?
Starting at 3 AM from Chennai allows you to reach Tirumala by 6–7 AM, before the main crowd arrives. Morning darshan queues are significantly shorter. For free darshan, early arrival is essential to avoid 8–10 hour waits. The highway is smooth and empty at this hour, and our experienced drivers are comfortable with early-morning drives on the Chennai–Tirupati route.
What should I carry for Tirupati darshan?
Must carry: ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN), TTD darshan ticket printout (if SED), minimal cash. Do not carry: mobile phones, cameras, and all electronic gadgets — they are strictly prohibited inside the temple. Leave them in the car with our driver or use TTD locker facilities. Wear traditional attire — no jeans, shorts, sleeveless tops, or printed T-shirts.
Is an inter-state permit required for the Chennai to Tirupati trip?
Yes. Since Tirupati is in Andhra Pradesh and our vehicles are registered in Tamil Nadu, an inter-state permit is mandatory. We arrange the permit in advance so you don't have to worry about it. The permit cost (~₹500–800 depending on vehicle type) is charged as an extra and is included in the cost breakdown we share before booking confirmation.
Can I cover Tirupati, Srikalahasti and Kanipakam in one day?
Technically possible but not recommended. Tirumala darshan alone takes 2–4 hours (with SED), and you need time for the ghat road drive. Adding Srikalahasti (40 km) and Kanipakam (70 km further) would make it an exhausting 18+ hour day. Our 2-day package from ₹6,500 gives you a comfortable pace with an overnight stay near Tirupati.
