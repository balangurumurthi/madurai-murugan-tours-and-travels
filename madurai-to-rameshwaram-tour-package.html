Places covered

Places to visit in Rameswaram on our Madurai to Rameshwaram tour

Here's everything we cover on the 1-day and 2-day Madurai to Rameswaram package. Skip any, add others — the itinerary is flexible and built around your interests.

01

Arulmigu Ramanathaswamy Temple

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas and a Char Dham site. Famous for the world's longest temple corridor (1,212 m) and 22 sacred theerthams (wells) inside the complex. The spiritual heart of every Madurai to Rameswaram trip.

Must visit ~2 hrs
02

Agniteertham — the sacred sea

Just 100 m east of the temple. Where Lord Rama is said to have washed away his sin of slaying Ravana. Pilgrims take a holy dip here before darshan. Calm, shallow shore, very safe for families.

Must visit ~30 min
03

Pamban Bridge & New Vertical Lift Rail Bridge

India's first sea bridge — 2.06 km over the Palk Strait — and the new vertical-lift rail bridge that runs parallel to it. We stop for 10–15 minutes for photos. One of the most photographed spots in Rameswaram.

Must visit ~20 min
04

Dhanushkodi ghost town

The eerie remains of a town wiped out by the 1964 cyclone. Ruined church, broken railway station, golden beach with two oceans meeting. 18 km from Rameswaram — unmissable on the 2-day package.

2-day package ~2 hrs
05

Arichal Munai — land's end

The southern-most drivable point of Dhanushkodi where the Bay of Bengal meets the Indian Ocean. Sri Lanka is just 31 km from here. Stunning, windswept, photogenic.

2-day package ~45 min
06

Kothandaramaswamy Temple

12 km from the main temple at the southern tip. The only structure to survive the 1964 cyclone. Beautiful seaside location with deer often visible. Quick 30-minute visit, calm and uncrowded.

Must visit ~30 min
07

Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Memorial

Dedicated to India's beloved President and former resident of Rameswaram. A peaceful, well-maintained museum with personal artifacts, awards and a life-size statue. Free entry, family-friendly.

Must visit ~45 min
08

Gandhamadhana Parvatham

The highest point in Rameswaram. A small temple hill with Lord Rama's footprint enshrined inside. Panoramic 360° view of the island and the sea. Best at sunrise or sunset.

Optional ~30 min
09

Five-Faced Hanuman Temple

Famous for the floating stones believed to be from Ram Setu — the bridge built by Lord Rama's army. A quick, devotional stop near the main temple. Especially loved by children.

Optional ~20 min
10

Ramanathapuram — Ramalinga Vilasam Palace

On the way back, an optional stop at Ramanathapuram (55 km from Rameswaram). The 17th-century Sethupathi palace has remarkable Tamil murals depicting the Ramayana. Quick 45-min visit.

Optional ~45 min
11

Sphatika (crystal) Lingam darshan

A rare and special darshan that happens daily at 5:00 AM only inside the main temple. A small additional ticket is required. Recommended only on the 2-day package with overnight stay.

2-day package ~20 min
12

Local market & conch-shell shopping

The vibrant market street around Ramanathaswamy temple sells conch shells, pearl jewellery, sea-shell handicrafts and Rameswaram-special vibhuti. A nice 30-minute walk after darshan.

Optional ~30 min
Essential information

Ramanathaswamy temple darshan — essential information

Know before you go — the 22 theerthams, sphatika lingam, dress code and temple timings.

22 theerthams (sacred wells)

The temple has 22 sacred wells inside the complex, each with water believed to have healing properties. The tradition is to bathe in all 22 before having darshan. Allow 45 minutes to 1 hour for this. Carry an extra set of clothes — you will get wet.

Sphatika lingam darshan

The rare Sphatika (crystal) lingam darshan happens daily at 5:00 AM only. If you want this special darshan, opt for the 2-day package with overnight stay. A small additional ticket is required at the temple counter.

Dress code

Traditional attire preferred. Men: dhoti/veshti, or trousers with shirt — many men also wear bare-chested (with shawl) inside. Women: saree, churidar or salwar. Avoid: shorts, sleeveless tops, very short skirts. Footwear is removed at the gate.

Temple timings & phone rules

Temple is open 5 AM – 1 PM and 3 PM – 9 PM. Mobile phones and cameras are not allowed in the inner sanctum — use the locker at the entrance (₹20–₹50). Our driver will safeguard your bags in the car.

Route details

Madurai to Rameshwaram cab route & distance

170 km via NH 49

The fastest route from Madurai to Rameshwaram is via NH 49 (Kochi–Madurai–Tondi–Rameshwaram Highway). Well-maintained 4-lane road for most of the journey. Key towns en route: Manamadurai, Paramakudi, Ramanathapuram.

3 to 4 hours drive

Madurai to Rameshwaram typically takes 3 to 3.5 hours driving. We allow an extra 30 minutes for a Pamban Bridge photo stop. Total one-way time including breaks: approximately 4 hours.

Pamban Bridge crossing

The 2.06 km Pamban Bridge connects mainland India to Rameshwaram island. Sea on both sides, often dolphins visible. The new vertical-lift rail bridge alongside is engineering art. All tourist vehicles allowed 24/7 — no special permit needed.

What is the dress code for Ramanathaswamy Temple?
Traditional attire is preferred. Men: dhoti/veshti, or trousers with a shirt — many men also wear bare-chested with a shawl inside. Women: saree, churidar or salwar kameez. What is the best time to visit Rameshwaram from Madurai?
October to March is the most pleasant time for a Madurai to Rameshwaram trip — cool weather, calm sea and clear views of the Pamban Bridge. Avoid the summer months of April to June when temperatures can cross 38°C. Monsoon (October–November) is also fine but Dhanushkodi may be wet and windy.
Is Pamban Bridge open for tourist vehicles?
Yes. The Pamban road bridge connects mainland India to Rameshwaram island and is open 24/7 for all tourist vehicles — no special permit required. The new Pamban Vertical Lift Rail Bridge runs parallel and offers excellent photo opportunities. Our drivers always stop for 10–15 minutes so you can take photos.
